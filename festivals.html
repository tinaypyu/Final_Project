Festivals in Taiwan

Taiwan has a lot of different festivals, many of which TWSA celebrates through events! Continue reading to learn a little bit more about festivals in Taiwan and what people do during these festivals.

Lunar New Year

Lunar New Year is the start of a new year on the Lunar Calendar, which usually happens in February. Family members who have moved out of their homes come back to spend the holiday with each other. It is a very important holiday in Chinese culture. Food, of course, is a big part of Lunar New Year, especially those that symbolizes luck and prosperity. Children receive red envelopes with money during Lunar New Year. This year is the Year of the Rabbit.

Qingming Festival

Qingming Festival, also known as Tomb Sweeping Festival, happens in April each year. It is usually the 4th, 5th , or 6th of April. Like what the name suggest, it is a day where people remember and honor their ancestors. They visit the graves of their ancestors with food and clean the gravesites.

Dragon Boat Festival

Dragon Boat Festival is also a holiday on the lunar calendar (5th day of the 5th month), and it usually takes place in June. This holiday commemorates the death of Qu Yuan, a ancient Chinese poet. On this day, people eat zongzi, a type of traditional Chinese dumplings. Dragon boat racing is also a popular activity for this holiday.

Mid Autumn Festival

Mid Autumn Festival is a holiday that takes place in the 8th month on the lunar calendar, and it usually happens in September. Similar holidays are also celebrated in Korea, Japan, Vietnam and other East/ Southeast Asian countries. On this day, the moon is full and bright. People carry around lanterns and eat mooncakes. In recetly years, traditional BBQ has also become a popoular activity for Mid Autumn Festival
